Data

Data from two sources mentioned below was used for this study.
  1. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)

    2. Air quality Index for US by county from 2015-2019.

  2. Census Data

    3. Median Household Income by county from 2015-2019.

Data collected from EPA for Air Quality Index was in county level and overall records for 1842 counties throughout the United States was present on the data. Data collected from census website had records of Median Household Income for 3221 counties. For this study, records of Median Household Income and AQI for 1842 counties was considered after dropping counties that do not have AQI values. Also, since the Median Household Income value collected from census data was average value for five years and AQI values collected from EPA was just for one year, we averaged out the value of AQI for five consecutive years from 2015 to 2019 and and used that value for analysis.

A new field (Low-Income) was also used for the data analysis in this study. Value of Low-Income was either true or false. We considered Low-Income to be true if Median Household Income for that county is less than the average Median Household Income value of the whole United States.
