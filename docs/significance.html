Why is the study important?

Consumption of polluted air leads to harmful effects on the health of people. While there is no doubt about that, it is also important to plan for a better economy, education and other socio economic factors. Now the question is while aiming to speed up economic growth should we be cautious about the effects of these activities on the environment. It is known that industrial processes emit huge amounts of organic compounds carbon monoxide, hydrocarbons, and chemicals into the air. These pollutants are reasons for global warming and greenhouse effect in the air. Burning gas, coal and oil results in three times as many deaths as road traffic accidents worldwide and it is estimated that air pollution has a $2.9 trillion economic cost, equating to 3.3 percent of the worldâ€™s GDP.

So various socio-economic factors contribute to the increase in air pollution and also we can find some patterns in levels of air pollution based on socio economic factors.

This study of understanding relationship between socio-economic factors and exposure to air pollution is important because it helps in more in-detail understanding of how socio economic factors are related with air pollution that can help autorities to come up with an appropriate plan for sustainable developmental activities.

Air pollution affects people and society in numerous ways. People with lower socio-economic status are at even riskier side of getting severely hurt because they are less aware about negative effects of air pollution and have limited resources for proper health care.

Some well known impacts of air pollution includes,

  1. Impacts of residents

    2. Tiny particles in atmosphere can easily penetrate through human respiratory system and cause damage to cardiovascular system. It has both short as well as long-term impacts on human health.

  2. Impacts on local farmers

    3. It reduces crop yields and life of plants.

  3. Impacts on global climate

    4. Green house gases present in the atmosphere is strongly linked to sea level rise and global warming.

  4. Impacts on aviation agencies

    5. Reduced visibility causes flight cancellations, diversions, and delays in reaching destinations.

  5. Impacts on economy

    6. Costs related to human health increase and productivity decreases due to poor health of people.
